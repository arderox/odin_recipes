Lasagna

Description

This dish is a traditional mexican plate.

Really anyone can make it, it consists of traditional mexican ingredients layered over the top of one another.

Although it shares it's name with lasagna it has little similarity, so lasagna haters have no fear!

Ingredients

  • One and half pounds ground beef.
  • One fourth of a cup of water.
  • One packet or one ounce of taco seasoning mix.
  • Twelve flour tortillas, or more if needed.
  • One can of refried beans.
  • Three cups of shredded Colby-Jack cheese.
  • One jar of taco sauce.
  • One cup of shredded Colby-Jack cheese.

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Grease a 9x13-inch casserole dish
  2. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat and stir in ground beef. Cook and stir until beef is crumbly, evenly browned, and no longer pink, about 10 minutes. Frain and discard any excess grease. Add water and taco seasoning; cook and strin until mixture has thickened, 5 to 10 minutes.
  3. Arrange 3 tortillas in the bottom of the casserole dish; spread about half the can of redried beans and half the ground beef atop the tortilla layer. Sprinke 1 cup Colby-Jack cheese over ground beef layer. Arrange 3 more tortillas over the cheese. Spread in the remaining half can refried beans and half the jar of taco sauce; sprinkle with 1 cup Colby-Jack cheese. Arrange 3 more tortillas and top with remaining ground beef and 1 cup Colby-Jack cheese. Top with last 3 tortillas and remaining half jar taco sauce
  4. Bake in preheated oven until top tortilla layer begins to brown, 20 to 25 minutes. Sprinkle 1 cup Colby-Jack cheese over lasagna.
  5. Turn on oven's broiler
  6. Place lasagna under the broiler until Colby-Jack cheese is melted and bubbling, 3 to 5 minutes.
